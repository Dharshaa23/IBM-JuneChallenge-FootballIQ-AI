# Football Knowledge Base (Sample Excerpts)

This file is a condensed, sample knowledge base standing in for the full FIFA
"Laws of the Game" PDF. In production, replace this with the official PDF and
the Docling ingestion pipeline in `utils/rag.py` will parse it automatically
(Docling can ingest PDF, DOCX, HTML, and images alike).

## Law 11 - Offside

A player is in an offside position if any part of the head, body, or feet is
in the opponents' half (excluding the halfway line) and closer to the
opponents' goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent, at the
moment the ball is played by a teammate. A player is not penalized for being
in an offside position alone -- they must also become involved in active play
by interfering with play, an opponent, or gaining an advantage.

## Law 12 - Fouls and Misconduct

Direct free kicks are awarded for careless, reckless, or excessive-force
fouls such as kicking, tripping, or pushing an opponent. A penalty kick is
awarded if such an offense occurs inside the offender's own penalty area.
Cautions (yellow cards) are issued for unsporting behavior, dissent, or
persistent infringement. A red card (dismissal) follows serious foul play,
violent conduct, denying an obvious goal-scoring opportunity through a
handball or foul, or receiving a second yellow card.

## Law 14 - The Penalty Kick

A penalty kick is awarded when a direct free-kick offense is committed by a
defending player inside their own penalty area. The ball is placed on the
penalty mark, and only the kicker and the goalkeeper are allowed inside the
penalty area when the kick is taken. The goalkeeper must have at least part
of one foot on or in line with the goal line when the kick is taken.

## Penalty Shootouts

When a knockout match is level after extra time, the result is decided by
kicks from the penalty mark. Each team takes alternating kicks; if scores are
level after five kicks each, the shootout continues in sudden-death pairs
until one team has scored more from an equal number of kicks.

## Own Goals

An own goal is recorded when a player inadvertently or deliberately puts the
ball into their own team's goal. It counts toward the opposing team's score
in the match result, but is typically tracked separately from a player's
personal goal tally in statistics.

## Extra Time and Away Goals

Many knockout competitions use 30 minutes of extra time (two 15-minute
halves) when scores are level after 90 minutes, followed by a penalty
shootout if still level. The away-goals rule, once common in two-legged
ties, has been phased out by UEFA and several other competitions in recent
years in favor of simple aggregate scoring plus extra time/penalties.

## Substitutions

Most modern competitions allow up to five substitutions per team during
normal time (often in three stoppages, plus halftime), with an additional
substitution permitted if the match goes to extra time. Substitutions are
used tactically to change formation, freshen up tired legs, replace an
injured or suspended-risk player, or alter momentum.

## Formations and Tactics (Glossary)

- **Possession**: The percentage of total play time a team controls the ball.
- **Pressing**: Coordinated effort to win the ball back quickly high up the pitch.
- **Transition**: The phase of play immediately after winning or losing possession.
- **Low block**: A defensive shape where a team sits deep to deny space behind the defense.
- **Overload**: Creating a numerical advantage in a specific zone of the pitch.
- **Counter-attack**: A fast attacking move launched immediately after regaining possession.

## Tournament Formats

- **Group stage**: Teams are divided into groups and play each other once or twice; top teams advance.
- **Knockout stage**: Single-elimination matches; losers are eliminated.
- **Neutral venue**: A match played in a country/stadium that is not the home ground of either team, common in major tournament finals or shootout-deciding fixtures.
